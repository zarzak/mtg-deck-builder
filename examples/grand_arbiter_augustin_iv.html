Strategy

Grand Arbiter Augustin IV is the quintessential stax/tax commander, simultaneously reducing your own spell costs while making every opponent spell cost one more mana. The deck aims to layer additional tax effects on top of the commander to create a prison-style lock where opponents cannot function, while you advance your board with discounted spells. Win conditions typically involve grinding opponents out through resource denial or assembling a combo under the protection of the tax shell.

Key Mechanics

cost reduction for white spells, cost reduction for blue spells, tax opponents, stax, control, prison

Synergy Keywords

Evaluation Notes

Cards that tax or restrict opponents are dramatically elevated in value — even weak or narrow stax pieces become excellent because they stack multiplicatively with the commander. High-CMC spells are better than normal because the cost reduction makes them cheaper, so expensive haymakers are more playable. Counterspells are especially strong since opponents must pay the tax before the counter even fires. Cards that produce incremental advantage over many turns (artifacts, enchantments that generate value) are better than burst-value cards. Mana rocks are highly valued because they accelerate past the tax you impose on yourself. Conversely, cards that rely on opponents doing things (group hug, politics) are anti-synergistic.
